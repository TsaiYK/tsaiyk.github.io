Research Area

Control Co-Design for
Intelligent Autonomous Systems

We advance control co-design (CCD) methodologies that integrate reinforcement learning (RL) and model predictive control (MPC) to jointly optimize physical design and control policies for autonomous systems operating in dynamic and uncertain environments.

Overview

Traditional engineering design separates physical system design from controller design, solving each sequentially. This sequential approach is inherently suboptimal because a system's physical structure fundamentally shapes what controllers can achieve, and vice versa. Control Co-Design (CCD) breaks this barrier by solving both problems simultaneously, unlocking performance gains that neither approach can achieve alone.

Our research develops CCD formulations that are scalable, robust, and adaptable. We integrate deep reinforcement learning to handle high-dimensional, nonlinear design-control spaces, and tube-based Model Predictive Control to guarantee constraint satisfaction under bounded disturbances. We also develop parametric and multi-objective formulations that yield a rich set of Pareto-optimal design-control tradeoffs.

Application domains include active suspension systems, robotics, aerospace, and smart material structures. A key focus is enabling multi-generation CCD — where digital twins accumulate operational data to improve the joint design and policy in successive system iterations.

Key Research Themes

Simultaneous & Nested CCD Formulations

We develop both simultaneous formulations (solving design and control jointly) and nested formulations (iterating between the two), identifying when each is preferable and how to bridge them efficiently.

RL-Based CCD for Nonlinear Systems

We leverage deep reinforcement learning to jointly optimize design parameters and control policies for complex, nonlinear systems with high-dimensional state-action spaces, without requiring explicit dynamic models.

Robust CCD with Tube-MPC

We extend CCD to uncertain environments using tube-based MPC, ensuring that the jointly optimized system remains safe and performant even under bounded disturbances and model mismatch.
