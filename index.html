What we study

Research Areas

We develop fundamental frameworks for optimizing dynamic engineering systems through the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning (AI/ML), optimization, modern control, co-design, and digital twin technologies. We are building intelligent and adaptable systems that continuously learn from real-world data under uncertainty and evolve over their operational lifecycle with applications to autonomous systems across robotics, advanced manufacturing, automotive, smart materials, and aerospace domains.

AI-Enabled Digital Twins for Real-Time Decision-Making

We develop digital twin frameworks that fuse physics-based models with ML to enable real-time monitoring, control, and uncertainty-aware decision-making throughout a system's lifecycle.

View related papers →

Control Co-Design for Intelligent Autonomous Systems

We advance CCD methodologies that integrate reinforcement learning (RL) and model predictive control (MPC) to jointly optimize physical design and control policies for autonomous systems operating in dynamic and uncertain environments.

View related papers →

Multi-Fidelity Learning and Optimization under Uncertainty

We develop multi-fidelity active learning and Bayesian optimization methods that scale to system-level digital twins with inherent noises, enabling data-efficient and interpretable decision-making across large-scale engineering networks.

Prof. Ying-Kuan Tsai

Principal Investigator

Dr. Tsai's research focuses on optimizing dynamic engineering systems, with an emphasis on integrating AI/ML, optimization, modern control, and digital twins. He has published over 20 peer-reviewed papers in the fields of engineering design, optimization, manufacturing, control, and digital twins. Before joining Texas State University, Dr. Tsai was a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Northwestern University
