What we study

Research Areas

We develop fundamental frameworks for optimizing dynamic engineering systems through the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning (AI/ML), optimization, modern control, co-design, and digital twin technologies. We are building intelligent and adaptable systems that continuously learn from real-world data under uncertainty and evolve over their operational lifecycle with applications to autonomous systems across robotics, advanced manufacturing, automotive, smart materials, and aerospace domains.

AI-Enabled Digital Twins for Real-Time Decision-Making

We develop digital twin frameworks that fuse physics-based models with ML to enable real-time monitoring, control, and uncertainty-aware decision-making throughout a system's lifecycle.

Learn more →

Control Co-Design for Intelligent Autonomous Systems

We advance CCD methodologies that integrate reinforcement learning (RL) and model predictive control (MPC) to jointly optimize physical design and control policies for autonomous systems operating in dynamic and uncertain environments.

Learn more →

Active Learning and Optimization under Uncertainty

We develop active learning and advanced optimization methods that scale to system-level digital twins with inherent noise for data-efficient and interpretable decision-making across large-scale engineering systems.

Learn more →

Control Systems

Mechanical Engineering · Texas State University

This course covers introductory concepts of linear control systems.

MEEN 357 Fall 2023

Engineering Analysis for Mechanical Engineers

Undergraduate Course · Mechanical Engineering · Texas A&M University

This course provides a practical foundation for the use of numerical methods to solve engineering problems.

  • Topics: solving systems of linear equations, curve fitting, root finding, numerical integration, numerical differentiation, initial value problems, boundary value problems, eigenvalues, and optimization methods.
  • Project: Apply numerical methods and Python programming to model, simulate, and optimize an autonomous Mars rover capable of navigating challenging Martian terrain.
