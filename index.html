What we study

Research Areas

Our work sits at the intersection of cognitive science, machine learning, and neuroscience. We ask questions that neither field can answer alone.

Memory & Representation

We probe how episodic and semantic memories are structured in the hippocampal-neocortical circuit, and how analogous representations emerge in transformer-based models.

View related papers →

Causal Reasoning

How do humans and models distinguish correlation from causation? We design experiments and benchmarks to probe causal inference under uncertainty.

View related papers →

Cognitive Benchmarking

We build rigorous evaluation suites that test reasoning, generalization, and robustness — exposing where current AI systems diverge from human cognition.

View related papers →

Language & Grounding

Language is not a symbol system — it is grounded in perception and action. We study how meaning is anchored in the world, in both children and large language models.

View related papers →
